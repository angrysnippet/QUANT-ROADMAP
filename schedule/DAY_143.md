## Block 1 — Volatility clustering (Mandelbrot's observation)
"Large changes tend to be followed by large changes, of either sign; small by small." Calm begets calm, storms beget storms. Formally: returns are *uncorrelated* but *not independent* — `|rₜ|` and `rₜ²` are strongly autocorrelated with slow decay.

*Why it matters:* this is the rare free lunch of predictability. You can't forecast tomorrow's *return*, but you can forecast tomorrow's *risk* — and that's enough to size positions, price options, and manage drawdowns.

---

## Block 2 — Measuring it: realized vol, EWMA, and the √t scaling
- **Realized volatility:** `σ̂ = std(returns)` over a window; annualize by `×√252` (daily) — the `√t` law from Day 140.
- **Equally-weighted** windows react too slowly and have a "ghost"/plateau effect (a big day stays in the window then drops out abruptly).
- **EWMA (RiskMetrics):** weight recent data more, `σ²ₜ = λσ²ₜ₋₁ + (1−λ)r²ₜ₋₁`, typically `λ=0.94` daily. Smooth, responsive, one parameter, no plateau artifact.

**Concept check:** why annualize with `√252` and not `252`? (Variance scales with time; standard deviation with √time — independent increments, Day 140.)

---

## Block 3 — GARCH(1,1): the workhorse model
GARCH lets variance be *persistent and mean-reverting*:
```
σ²ₜ = ω + α·r²ₜ₋₁ + β·σ²ₜ₋₁
```
- `α` = reaction to the last shock (how jumpy),
- `β` = persistence of variance (how long storms last),
- `α+β` ≈ persistence; `< 1` for stationarity. Long-run variance = `ω/(1−α−β)`.

EWMA is just GARCH with `ω=0, α+β=1` (no mean reversion). Real markets have `α+β ≈ 0.95–0.99` — very persistent but ultimately mean-reverting. **EGARCH/GJR** add the *leverage effect*: down moves raise vol more than up moves (fear > greed).

---

## Block 4 — Realized vs Implied, and what it buys you
- **Realized vol:** backward-looking, from actual returns.
- **Implied vol:** forward-looking, *backed out of option prices* (Day 140's GBM σ). The **VIX** is roughly 30-day implied vol on the S&P.
- **Variance risk premium:** implied usually trades *above* subsequent realized — option sellers earn a premium for bearing crash risk. Systematically harvesting it works… until it spectacularly doesn't (short-vol blowups). Respect the fat left tail (Day 119).

---

## Block 5 — Quant Thinking · vol targeting (the practical payoff)
If you can forecast σ, you can **target constant risk**: scale position so `position × σ̂ = target`. Lever up in calm, cut in storms. This single idea:
- stabilizes the *risk* of a strategy (so the Sharpe — Day 145 — is comparable through time),
- mechanically reduces exposure *before* the worst drawdowns (vol rises first),
- underlies risk parity and most CTA/managed-futures programs.

**Hard puzzle:** two strategies have the same average return, but A has stable vol and B has wild vol clustering. Why does vol-targeting B improve its *compound* growth even though it doesn't change the arithmetic mean? (Volatility drag, Day 119/140: `g ≈ μ − ½σ²`; cutting variance in the storms lifts geometric return — the −½σ² term shrinks.)

**Career connection:** "what's actually predictable in markets?" → *not the mean, but the variance.* Mentioning clustering → EWMA/GARCH → vol targeting → the variance risk premium is a complete, senior-sounding arc.

## Block 6 — Python (quant) · EWMA vol and a GARCH(1,1) fit
```python
import numpy as np, pandas as pd
rng = np.random.default_rng(7)
# simulate clustered returns with a simple GARCH(1,1) DGP
T, omega, alpha, beta = 3000, 1e-6, 0.08, 0.90
r = np.zeros(T); s2 = np.full(T, omega/(1-alpha-beta))
for t in range(1, T):
    s2[t] = omega + alpha*r[t-1]**2 + beta*s2[t-1]
    r[t]  = np.sqrt(s2[t]) * rng.standard_normal()

# EWMA volatility (RiskMetrics, lambda=0.94)
lam = 0.94; ew = np.zeros(T); ew[0] = r.var()
for t in range(1, T):
    ew[t] = lam*ew[t-1] + (1-lam)*r[t-1]**2
ann = np.sqrt(ew*252)
print("EWMA annualized vol, last 3:", ann[-3:].round(3))

# fit a real GARCH(1,1) and recover parameters
from arch import arch_model                      # pip install arch
res = arch_model(r*100, vol="Garch", p=1, q=1, mean="Zero").fit(disp="off")
print(res.params[["alpha[1]", "beta[1]"]].round(3).to_dict(),
      " persistence:", round(res.params['alpha[1]']+res.params['beta[1]'], 3))
```
You'll recover `α≈0.08, β≈0.90`, persistence ≈ 0.98 — the model *learns* the clustering you built in.

## Quant Thinking Track — Pattern Recognition
The deep pattern: **uncorrelated ≠ independent.** Zero linear autocorrelation in returns coexists with rich nonlinear structure in their magnitudes. Whenever a "no signal" result appears, ask: no signal in *what functional* — the level, the absolute value, the square, the sign?
