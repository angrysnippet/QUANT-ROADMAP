## Block 1 — The foundations
- **Sample space** `Ω`: all outcomes. **Event** `A ⊆ Ω`.
- **Axioms:** `0 ≤ P(A) ≤ 1`, `P(Ω)=1`, and for disjoint events `P(A∪B)=P(A)+P(B)`.
- **Complement:** `P(Aᶜ) = 1 − P(A)` — often the easy route ("at least one" = 1 − "none").
- **Inclusion–exclusion:** `P(A∪B) = P(A)+P(B) − P(A∩B)`.

*Why it matters:* sloppy probability is the #1 source of wrong quant answers; the axioms are your error-check.

---

## Block 2 — Conditional probability & independence
```
P(A | B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B)
```
**Independence:** `P(A ∩ B) = P(A)·P(B)` ⇔ `P(A|B) = P(A)`. Don't confuse *independent* with *mutually exclusive* (exclusive events are **dependent** — if one happens the other can't).

**Concept check:** two fair dice — are "first die = 6" and "sum = 7" independent? (Compute: `P(sum=7)=1/6`, `P(sum=7 | first=6)=1/6` → independent. Surprising but true.)

---

## Block 3 — Bayes' theorem (the quant workhorse)
```
P(H | E) = P(E | H) · P(H) / P(E)
```
**Classic test puzzle:** a disease has prevalence 1%. A test is 99% sensitive and 99% specific. You test positive — `P(disease | +)`? Use a population of 10,000: 100 sick (99 test +), 9,900 healthy (99 test +). So `P(disease|+) = 99 / (99+99) = 50%`. **Base rates dominate** — the lesson behind countless false "signals."

## Block 4 — Common distributions (know these cold)
| Distribution | Models | Mean | Variance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bernoulli(p) | one yes/no trial | p | p(1−p) |
| Binomial(n,p) | # successes in n trials | np | np(1−p) |
| Geometric(p) | trials until first success | 1/p | (1−p)/p² |
| Poisson(λ) | rare events per interval | λ | λ |
| Uniform(a,b) | flat continuous | (a+b)/2 | (b−a)²/12 |
| Exponential(λ) | wait time, memoryless | 1/λ | 1/λ² |
| Normal(μ,σ²) | sums/averages (CLT) | μ | σ² |

**Exercise:** expected number of fair-coin tosses to get the first head? (Geometric, `1/p = 2`.)

## Block 5 — Quant Thinking (Probability is the price of uncertainty)

- **Markets** are probability machines: a price is roughly a probability-weighted expectation of future value.
- **Memorylessness** (Exponential/Geometric) models "time until next trade/event" in microstructure.
- **Poisson** models order arrivals and rare jumps.
- **Normal** appears because returns aggregate many small shocks (CLT — Day 121's neighbour) — *but* real returns have **fat tails**, a critical caveat you'll revisit.

**Hard puzzle (Bayes in markets):** a signal flags "up day" correctly 60% of the time, but up-days are only 50% of days. After a flag, what's `P(up | flag)`? Set up the table; notice modest edges are still tradeable *if* repeated independently many times (Day 121).

**Career connection:** quant interviews are **dense with conditional-probability and Bayes puzzles** — the disease-test and two-dice traps appear constantly.

## Block 6 — Python (quant) · Simulate to verify
```python
import numpy as np
rng = np.random.default_rng(0)
# Verify P(sum=7 | first=6) by simulation
d1 = rng.integers(1,7, 1_000_000); d2 = rng.integers(1,7, 1_000_000)
mask = d1 == 6
print(np.mean((d1+d2 == 7)[mask]))   # ≈ 0.1667
```
Bonus: simulate the disease-test puzzle and confirm ≈50%.
