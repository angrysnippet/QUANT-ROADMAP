## Block 1 вҖ” Random walk (discrete)
Sum of i.i.d. steps: `SвӮң = SвӮңвӮӢвӮҒ + ОөвӮң`. Key facts: after `n` steps, mean 0 (if steps are symmetric), **variance grows linearly** (`Var = nПғВІ`), so spread grows like `ПғвҲҡn`. It's **Markov** (Day 137) and the discrete ancestor of Brownian motion.

*Why it matters:* the random walk is the baseline model of an unpredictable price вҖ” and "you can't predict the next step from the past" is the efficient-market hypothesis in one line.

---

## Block 2 вҖ” Martingales (fair games)
A process is a **martingale** if `E[SвӮңвӮҠвӮҒ | history] = SвӮң` вҖ” your best forecast of tomorrow is today. A fair game; no predictable drift.

**Optional stopping theorem (intuition):** you can't beat a fair game with a clever stopping rule. This kills many "guaranteed" betting/trading systems (e.g. martingale doubling вҖ” bounded by ruin).

**Concept check:** is a random walk with zero-mean steps a martingale? (Yes.) With positive drift? (No вҖ” it's a submartingale.)

---

## Block 3 вҖ” Brownian motion (continuous)
Take the random walk's step size вҶ’ 0 and steps вҶ’ вҲһ (scaled right) and you get **Brownian motion `W(t)`**: continuous, `W(0)=0`, independent increments, `W(t)вҲ’W(s) ~ Normal(0, tвҲ’s)`, nowhere differentiable. The mathematical heart of continuous-time finance.

**Key scaling:** standard deviation grows like **`вҲҡt`** вҖ” *time-scaling of volatility* (why vol annualises by `вҲҡ252`).

---

## Block 4 вҖ” Geometric Brownian Motion (the price model)
Prices can't go negative and grow multiplicatively, so model **returns** as Brownian:
```
dS = Ој S dt + Пғ S dW      вҶ’      S_T = SвӮҖ В· exp[ (Ој вҲ’ ВҪПғВІ)T + ПғвҲҡT В· Z ],  Z~N(0,1)
```
This is **GBM** вҖ” the model behind BlackвҖ“Scholes. Log-returns are Normal; prices are log-normal. The `вҲ’ВҪПғВІ` term is the **ItГҙ correction** (volatility drags down the median growth вҖ” a famous subtlety).

**Exercise:** why does higher Пғ *lower* the median terminal price even with the same Ој? (The `вҲ’ВҪПғВІ` drift correction вҖ” volatility eats compound growth; arithmetic vs geometric mean again, Day 119.)

## Block 5 вҖ” Quant Thinking (this is the foundation of pricing)

- **Brownian motion + risk-neutral drift** вҶ’ simulate `S_T` (you did this on Day 139) вҶ’ price options.
- **ItГҙ's lemma** (preview): the chain rule for stochastic processes; it gives the BlackвҖ“Scholes PDE. You don't need the full proof now вҖ” internalise that *functions of Brownian motion get an extra ВҪПғВІВ·(second-derivative) term.*
- **Real markets deviate:** volatility isn't constant (clustering вҶ’ GARCH, Day 143), jumps happen, tails are fat. GBM is the *baseline*, not the truth вҖ” know its assumptions and where they break.

**Hard puzzle:** if log-returns are Normal with mean 0, is the *expected price* unchanged over time? (No вҖ” `E[S_T] = SвӮҖe^(ОјT)` with the level drift; the median uses `ОјвҲ’ВҪПғВІ`. Confusing mean vs median price is a classic trap.)

**Career connection:** "what model do prices follow?" вҶ’ GBM, with log-normal prices, `вҲҡt` vol scaling, and the ItГҙ `вҲ’ВҪПғВІ` correction вҖ” *and* the caveats (constant-vol/no-jumps are false). That nuance is exactly what pricing interviews probe.

## Block 6 вҖ” Python (quant) В· Simulate GBM paths
```python
import numpy as np
rng = np.random.default_rng(0)
S0,mu,sigma,T,steps,paths = 100,0.05,0.2,1.0,252,10000
dt = T/steps
incr = (mu-0.5*sigma**2)*dt + sigma*np.sqrt(dt)*rng.standard_normal((paths,steps))
S = S0*np.exp(incr.cumsum(axis=1))
print("E[S_T] sim:", round(S[:,-1].mean(),2), " theory:", round(S0*np.exp(mu*T),2))
print("median S_T:", round(np.median(S[:,-1]),2),
      " theory:", round(S0*np.exp((mu-0.5*sigma**2)*T),2))
```
See mean vs median split exactly as the ItГҙ correction predicts.
